exp.o Visibility Reporting Redesign

Role: User Experience Lead at Expeditors

exp.o Visibility is a web application for Expeditors customers to track and report on their logistics data (shipments, orders, customs entries, inventory levels and electronic document images). Due to usability and performance issues, customers would often rely on Expeditors reps and support to provide the reports they needed (using an internal-only, legacy system), instead of using exp.o Visibility to self-serve the information. Rather getting the information they needed right away, it could sometimes take days.

The goal of this project was to transform the reporting tool in exp.o Visibility so that is usable and functional, enables customers to self-serve the information they need, and creates a positive user experience. We tackled this by employing UX design and research best practices, usability heuristics, proven interaction patterns, and the simplest possible workflows.

This was our approach, at a high-level:

  • Conduct an initial research sprint to validate assumptions about the system's usability. Methods used were analysis of user feedback and support tickets, contextual inquiry, and hueristic review.
  • Research and brainstorm improved reporting workflow.
  • Create and validate new workflow with a first-click test.
  • Create an initial, clickable prototype and conduct usability test to further validate workflow and form controls.
  • Refine prototype and conduct a second usability test.
  • Conduct a series of mini-studies to validate the workflow and controls around creating, running, and scheduling a report, and viewing a report's results.

At this point I left my role at Expeditors. The new workflow and some of the new features are currently in beta. The application isn't available to the public, but this is a demo video that showcases the functionality. There are also some screenshots in the help contents. I had no role in creating the video or the help contents.

Scenario 2
